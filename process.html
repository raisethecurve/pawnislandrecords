About Process

Creative Process

My music begins with intention.

Every track starts with words, mood, and emotional direction. Before there is sound, there is a feeling I want to reach, a tension I want to hold, or a world I want to open.

Project Page Current Release

Intention first. Judgment over output.

Method

The process is directed, tested, and repeatedly refined.

I work with generative audio tools as instruments: sources of sound, texture, variation, and surprise. But the process does not begin or end with output.

I direct it. I test it. I reject most of it. I keep shaping until something aligns with the emotional truth I am after.

Authorship

The tools provide raw material. I provide judgment, identity, and meaning.

  1. I write the lyrics.
  2. I define the structure.
  3. I choose the tone, pacing, and emotional arc.
  4. I decide what belongs and what gets discarded.

This is less like pressing a button and more like sculpting toward emotional truth.

Why It Matters

This is the method behind the catalog.

What matters is not how quickly something appears, but whether it carries weight, coherence, and feeling. That is what keeps the catalog unified by intention instead of output alone.
