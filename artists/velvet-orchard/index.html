Project Identity

World and Sound

Velvet Orchard is one of Pawn Island Records' most experimental pop-facing projects. It began in a bright bubblegum Y2K-pop lane, then expanded into a broader electronic world shaped by EDM, house, dubstep, bass music, club energy, and festival-inspired electronica.

That evolution is central to the project. Velvet Orchard should not be described as a static synth-pop identity. Its defining quality is restless experimentation: a willingness to move from candy-colored pop surfaces into heavier drops, club structures, dance-floor release, and large-scale electronic atmosphere.

Velvet Orchard is best framed as electronic pop through experimentation: melodic, dance-driven, emotionally charged, and increasingly shaped by the scale and force of modern EDM and festival culture.

Story Angles

  • Evolution from bubblegum Y2K pop into broader EDM and bass-facing electronica.
  • Club and festival inspiration filtered through emotional pop songwriting.
  • Bright surfaces, heavy drops, dance-floor release, and emotional excess.
