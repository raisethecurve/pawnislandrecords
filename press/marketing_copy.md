# Pawn Island Records — Project, Release, Press Kit, and Label Copy Stack

## Global Language Rules

Use **Pawn Island Records** in prose. Use **PAWN ISLAND RECORDS** only for logo, merch, masthead, or graphic-brand contexts.

Use these terms wherever possible:

* project
* project identity
* catalog project
* creative project
* release project
* catalog lane
* recording project
* public-facing project name
* press kit
* project page
* roster project

Avoid these terms wherever possible:

* artist
* band
* singer
* act
* performer
* real person
* fictional artist
* fake artist
* AI singer
* AI band
* virtual artist

Preferred public framing:

* These are creative music projects, similar in function to pen names, aliases, imprints, or personas.
* They are not presented as literal biographical people.
* Each project exists as a container for a genre lane, emotional world, sound palette, and release identity.
* Matt Freeman / Pawn Island Records supplies the human creative direction, writing, selection, arrangement, revision, and final approval.

Default project-page stance:

* Do not pretend the projects are literal people with invented hometowns, band members, childhoods, touring histories, or personal biographies.
* Do not foreground the generative process on every project page unless the page is specifically about process/authorship.
* Do not imply live performance, touring, radio history, press history, playlist adds, sync placements, charting, or personnel unless verified.

Approved process/authorship language:
Pawn Island Records is built around original lyrics, human creative direction, and intentional catalog design. The label uses modern production tools as part of its workflow, but the creative center remains the writing, selection, arrangement, revision, and final decisions that shape each release.

# Label / Founder Story

## 1-sentence label mission

Pawn Island Records is an independent creative music imprint building a genre-fluid catalog around original lyrics, atmosphere, story, and human-directed project identities.

## 75-word label bio

Pawn Island Records is an independent creative music imprint founded by Matt Freeman. The label develops genre-fluid project identities across electronic pop, hip-hop, singer-songwriter material, country blues, metal, stoner doom, pop-punk, and reflective spiritual writing. Its catalog is built around original lyrics, emotional clarity, atmosphere, and intentional worldbuilding. Rather than treating genre as a boundary, Pawn Island Records uses each project as a distinct container for songs with their own voice, mood, and purpose.

## Founder / label story

Pawn Island Records began as a way to give original writing a larger world to live in. Founded by poet, songwriter, and independent creator Matt Freeman, the label is built around the idea that songs can grow from language first: a title, a line, a memory, a spiritual pressure, a character, or a feeling that needs its own atmosphere.

Instead of forcing every song under one name or one genre, Pawn Island Records operates as a creative imprint with multiple project identities. Velvet Orchard carries the experimental electronic-pop lane, evolving from bubblegum Y2K pop into EDM, house, dubstep, bass, club, and festival-inspired electronica. Rhea Mauro holds the soul-rooted singer-songwriter lane. Quiet Filter moves through bluesy, reverb-soaked stoner doom. Resunant pushes into alternative power metal and narrative-driven glam rock. Crick Kinnard brings Appalachian blues and country grit. Blackout State channels pop-punk refusal. High Ground holds the hip-hop, rap, and boom-bap lane. second watch carries reflective, devotional-adjacent material. Matt Freeman remains the founder/writer identity at the center of the system.

The catalog is genre-fluid, but not random. Each project exists because a certain kind of song needs a specific world around it. Pawn Island Records is about emotional clarity, atmosphere, authorship, and building a body of work that can move between styles without losing its center.

At its core, the label is a home for songs shaped by intention: written, directed, selected, arranged, revised, and finalized under human creative control.

## Public process/authorship stance — quiet version

Pawn Island Records is built around original lyrics, human creative direction, and intentional catalog design. The label uses modern production tools as part of its workflow, but the creative center remains the writing, selection, arrangement, revision, and final decisions that shape each release.

## Public process/authorship stance — transparent version for process page only

Pawn Island Records uses generative audio tools as production instruments within a human-led creative process. The tools are not treated as authors or owners. Songs begin with human intention: lyrics, titles, themes, emotional direction, structure, selection, arrangement, revision, and final approval. The project identities function like creative containers, pen names, or personas: they organize the catalog by sound, tone, and world, but they are not presented as literal biographical people.

The label’s position is simple: technology can help realize a sound, but authorship belongs to the human creative work that directs, shapes, and finalizes the result.

## 3 principles

1. **Language first.** The catalog begins with lyrics, titles, emotional premises, and story.
2. **Worlds, not placeholders.** Every project should have its own sound, visual lane, and emotional logic.
3. **Human creative control.** Tools may assist production, but authorship, taste, selection, and final direction remain human.
